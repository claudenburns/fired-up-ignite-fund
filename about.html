Our Story

The Fired Up Ignite Fund was born from the belief that passion — real, relentless, bone-deep passion — deserves to be recognized and rewarded.

In Honor of Steven Lindsay

Steven Lindsay was a teacher and coach who had a rare gift: he made people genuinely believe in themselves. His energy was contagious in the best way — the kind that stuck with you long after class was over, long after the season ended.

He championed the students who didn't always fit the mold. The ones who were obsessed with something unexpected, who stayed late not because they had to, but because they couldn't imagine stopping. He saw something in those people that others sometimes missed, and he made sure they knew it.

The Fired Up Ignite Fund exists to honor that instinct — to find those same people and give them the fuel to keep going.

What We're Trying to Do

Most scholarship programs are built around academic performance, financial need, or a checklist of activities. We don't think those criteria capture the full picture of who deserves support.

Our mission is simple: find young people between the ages of 17 and 25 who are deeply, consistently, undeniably committed to something they love — and invest in them. The field doesn't matter. The grades don't matter. What matters is the fire.

Through an annual fundraiser and donor support, we offer grants and scholarships that give recipients room to take their work to the next level, without strings attached.

How the Fund Works

Annual fundraiser. The fund is sustained by donors who share the belief that passion deserves investment. Every year, we raise the capital to award the next class of recipients.

Open applications. Anyone ages 17–25 can apply. We ask about your craft, your commitment, and what you'd do with the support — not your GPA or extracurricular list.

Review and selection. Applications are reviewed by our committee with a single lens: is this person genuinely fired up about something? Do they have a clear direction for what comes next?

Flexible grants. Recipients receive financial support they can direct toward what they actually need — equipment, training, materials, travel, education, or whatever takes their work forward.
